## Why the Gap Exists

### 1. No Platform Infrastructure

In North America, CJ Affiliate and Rakuten have operated since the late 1990s. In Europe, Awin and Tradedoubler have been active for 20+ years. In MENA, **no comparable platform has emerged**.

The result:
- Brands manage affiliate relationships via **spreadsheets, WhatsApp, and email**
- Publishers sign up on **global platforms that don't support MENA currencies, languages, or payment methods**
- Tracking is **manual or uses generic tools** not optimized for regional attribution
- There is **no marketplace** where brands discover publishers and vice versa

### 2. Payment Infrastructure Challenges

Publishers in MENA face unique payment challenges:
- **Cross-border payments** between GCC countries are complex
- **Local payment methods** (bank transfers, CliQ, mada, Fawry) aren't supported by global platforms
- **Payment delays** of 60–120 days are common — some publishers wait 6+ months
- **Currency conversion** creates friction for multi-market campaigns
- **Regulatory requirements** differ by country (UAE, KSA, Egypt, Jordan all have different rules)

This makes publishers reluctant to invest in affiliate content creation — why spend time creating content if you might not get paid for months?

### 3. Language and Localization

Global affiliate platforms are built for English-speaking markets:
- **No Arabic-first interface** exists on any major platform
- **Arabic content optimization** is ignored — RTL layouts, Arabic NLP, regional SEO
- **Cultural nuances** in shopping behavior are missed (Ramadan shopping spikes, Friday vs Sunday patterns, modesty-focused fashion)
- **Local consumer behavior** is fundamentally different from Western markets

### 4. Fraud Patterns Are Different

MENA has unique fraud patterns that global detection systems miss:
- **VPN masking** — users appear to be in-market but aren't
- **Click farms** — organized fraud operations in specific MENA geographies
- **Bot traffic** — MENA-specific bot signatures differ from Western patterns
- **Cookie stuffing** — prevalent in coupon ecosystems
- **Geo-arbitrage** — exploiting different commission rates across MENA markets

Global fraud models, trained primarily on US/EU data, miss **40–60% of MENA-specific fraud**. This means brands lose money and lose trust in the affiliate channel.

### 5. Brand Awareness of Affiliate Marketing

Most MENA marketing teams are familiar with:
- Google Ads (SEM)
- Facebook/Instagram Ads
- Influencer marketing (flat-fee sponsorships)

But **80% of MENA brands** have never run a structured affiliate program because:
- They don't know what it is or that it exists as a channel
- They have no easy way to start (no platform, no publishers, no tracking)
- They perceive it as "coupon marketing" (discount-focused only)
- Their agencies don't offer affiliate services (because no platform exists)

### 6. Publisher Ecosystem Is Underdeveloped

In North America, there are tens of thousands of professional affiliate publishers. In MENA:
- **Coupon sites** exist but operate informally
- **Content publishers** create Arabic content but have no reliable monetization
- **Social media creators** with massive MENA audiences can't easily monetize via affiliate
- **Comparison sites** for MENA products/services are nascent
- **Cashback platforms** are just beginning to emerge

The chicken-and-egg problem: publishers won't invest in affiliate content without a reliable platform → brands won't invest in affiliate without enough publishers → neither side moves.
