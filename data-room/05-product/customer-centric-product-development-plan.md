# Perff AI — Customer-Centric Product Development Plan (Accelerator Phase)

> Scope: 3–4 month execution window (May–Aug 2026)
> Source baseline: data-room product, traction, and fundraising documents

---

## 1) Defined Customer Segments

| Segment | Profile | Primary Jobs-to-be-Done | Current Scale Signal |
|---|---|---|---|
| Enterprise Brands (Core) | Mid/large advertisers in UAE/KSA (e-commerce, food, fintech, real estate) | Acquire customers at predictable ROI; reduce paid-media waste; control fraud | 50+ active enterprise brands |
| Performance Publishers (Supply) | Coupon/content/social affiliates monetizing MENA audiences | Find high-converting campaigns; reliable tracking; fast payments | 870+ verified publishers, 450+ monthly active |
| Brand Growth Teams (Self-Serve Target) | Lean marketing teams needing speed and autonomy | Launch/manage campaigns without account-manager dependency | Self-serve roadmap priority (2026) |
| Ops/Finance Stakeholders | Internal teams managing payouts and reconciliation | Reduce payout errors/disputes; improve cash-flow predictability | 100% on-time payment reliability target |

---

## 2) Customer Needs and Pain Points

### Enterprise Brands
- Need: predictable CAC and clear attribution
- Pain: fragmented affiliate ops, manual onboarding, fraud exposure, slow optimization
- Opportunity: self-serve campaign setup + AI budget optimization + stronger fraud controls

### Publishers
- Need: transparent earnings and reliable payouts
- Pain: weak campaign matching, limited visibility, delayed approvals
- Opportunity: better campaign recommendations + faster onboarding + earnings clarity

### Growth Teams
- Need: faster launch cycle and lower operational overhead
- Pain: dependency on manual support for campaign setup/changes
- Opportunity: guided templates and self-serve controls

### Ops/Finance
- Need: clean payout operations and fewer disputes
- Pain: reconciliation friction across multiple publishers/campaigns
- Opportunity: payout workflow automation and exception alerts

| Stage | Customer Goal | Current Friction | Product Improvement (Accelerator) | Success Metric |
|---|---|---|---|---|

## 4) Product Hypotheses (Testable)

| Hypothesis | Why it matters | Experiment | Metric / Success Threshold |
|---|---|---|---|
| H1: Self-serve onboarding reduces launch friction | Faster activation should increase conversion to active campaigns | Roll out guided onboarding to 20 brands | Setup time reduced from baseline to <48h for 80% |
| H2: AI publisher recommendations improve early campaign outcomes | Better matching should improve first 30-day performance | A/B: AI recommendations vs manual shortlist | +15% CVR in AI cohort |
| H3: Fraud v2 reduces invalid conversions and payout disputes | Better trust and profitability | Deploy fraud v2 on top 30 campaigns | -20% invalid conversion rate; dispute rate down |
| H4: Template-based campaign creation increases repeat launches | More cadence from existing brands drives NRR | Launch vertical templates (retail/food/fintech) | +25% campaigns launched per active brand |

## 5) Prioritized Feature List (Accelerator Window)

### P0 (Must ship in 3–4 months)
1. Self-serve brand onboarding (wizard + validation)
2. Campaign templates by vertical
3. AI publisher recommendation v1
4. Fraud detection v2 (priority rules + anomaly scoring)
5. Unified reporting refresh (brand + publisher views)

### P1 (Should ship if P0 stable)
1. Webhook notifications for campaign events
2. Payout exception alerts and reconciliation improvements
3. Guided optimization suggestions in dashboard

### P2 (Next cycle candidate)
1. Public API beta hardening
2. Mobile publisher app enhancements
3. Multi-touch attribution advanced view

## 7) Operating Cadence

- Weekly: product KPI review (activation, CVR, fraud, time-to-launch)
- Bi-weekly: customer interviews (3 brands + 3 publishers)
- Monthly: roadmap checkpoint and reprioritization
- End-of-cycle: hypothesis review and next-quarter planning

---

## 8) Success Definition for Accelerator Phase

- Brand activation cycle measurably shorter (target <48h for most launches)
- Early campaign performance improves (+CVR and +ROAS)
- Fraud/quality outcomes improve (invalid conversion and dispute reduction)
- Self-serve usage proves readiness for broader SaaS transition
