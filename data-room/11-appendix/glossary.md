# Perff AI — Glossary of Terms

> **Affiliate Marketing & Industry Terms Explained**

---

## Affiliate Marketing Terms

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| **Affiliate Marketing** | A performance-based marketing model where brands pay publishers (affiliates) a commission for driving conversions (sales, leads, or actions) |
| **Affiliate Network** | A platform that connects brands with publishers, provides tracking technology, and manages payments. Perff AI is an affiliate network |
| **Publisher (Affiliate)** | An individual or company that promotes a brand's products/services and earns commissions on conversions. Examples: coupon sites, content bloggers, comparison platforms, social media influencers |
| **Brand (Advertiser / Merchant)** | A company that wants to sell products or services through affiliate partnerships |
| **Campaign** | A structured affiliate promotion with defined commission rates, creative assets, tracking parameters, and duration |
| **Conversion** | A desired action completed by a consumer — a sale, lead submission, app install, or other defined action |
| **Commission** | The amount paid to a publisher for each conversion they drive |
| **Take Rate** | The percentage of the total commission amount that the affiliate network (Perff AI) retains as revenue |

## Pricing Models

| Term | Definition | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **CPS (Cost Per Sale)** | Publisher earns a percentage of the sale value | Brand pays 10% of $100 sale = $10 commission |
| **CPA (Cost Per Action)** | Publisher earns a fixed amount per completed action | Brand pays $7 per app install |
| **CPL (Cost Per Lead)** | Publisher earns a fixed amount per qualified lead | Brand pays $150 per real estate inquiry |
| **CPC (Cost Per Click)** | Publisher earns per click (rare in affiliate marketing) | Brand pays $0.50 per click |
| **CPM (Cost Per Mille)** | Cost per 1,000 impressions (not used in affiliate marketing) | Brand pays $5 per 1,000 impressions |
| **Rev Share** | Publisher earns a percentage of ongoing revenue from referred customers | Publisher earns 20% of monthly subscription revenue |

## Performance Metrics

| Term | Definition | Good Benchmark |
|---|---|---|
| **CVR (Conversion Rate)** | Percentage of clicks that result in a conversion | 1–3% global avg; 8.2% Perff AI |
| **ROAS (Return on Ad Spend)** | Revenue generated per dollar spent on advertising | >4× is strong; 7.0× Perff AI |
| **AOV (Average Order Value)** | Average monetary value of each completed order | Varies by vertical |
| **EPC (Earnings Per Click)** | Revenue generated per click across all publishers | Higher = better publisher quality |
| **Click-Through Rate (CTR)** | Percentage of impressions that result in clicks | 2–5% typical |
| **Attribution** | The process of identifying which publisher or marketing touchpoint deserves credit for a conversion | — |
| **Multi-Touch Attribution** | Giving partial credit to multiple touchpoints in a consumer's journey | More accurate than last-click |
| **Last-Click Attribution** | Giving 100% credit to the last publisher the consumer clicked before converting | Simple but incomplete |
| **Postback / Server-Side Tracking** | Conversion tracking via server-to-server communication (not browser cookies) | More reliable than pixel tracking |
| **Pixel Tracking** | Conversion tracking via a code snippet on the brand's confirmation page | Less reliable due to ad blockers |

## Business Metrics

| Term | Definition | Perff AI Value |
|---|---|---|
| **ARR (Annual Recurring Revenue)** | Annualized revenue run rate | $1.8M (2025) |
| **MRR (Monthly Recurring Revenue)** | Monthly revenue | $150K (Jan 2026) |
| **NRR (Net Revenue Retention)** | Revenue from existing clients after expansion and churn, as a % of prior period | 120%+ |
| **LTV (Lifetime Value)** | Total gross profit from a client over the relationship | ~$67K per brand |
| **CAC (Customer Acquisition Cost)** | Total cost to acquire a new client | $8.5K per brand |
| **LTV/CAC** | Ratio of lifetime value to acquisition cost | 8× (excellent) |
| **Payback Period** | Months to recover CAC from client revenue | 5.3 months |
| **GMV (Gross Merchandise Value)** | Total value of transactions processed through the platform | $4.2M/month |
| **Churn Rate** | Percentage of clients lost per period | 13% annual |
| **Retention Rate** | Percentage of clients retained per period | 87% annual |
| **EBITDA** | Earnings Before Interest, Taxes, Depreciation, and Amortization | Operating profitability measure |
| **Gross Margin** | Revenue minus direct costs (COGS), as a percentage of revenue | 66.5% (2025) |
| **Burn Rate** | Monthly cash consumption (expenses minus revenue) | $30K/month (2025) |
| **Runway** | Months of operation possible with current cash | 28–36 months post-investment |

## Technology Terms

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| **AI/ML (Artificial Intelligence / Machine Learning)** | Technology that enables software to learn from data and make predictions without explicit programming |
| **NLP (Natural Language Processing)** | AI technology that enables computers to understand and generate human language — critical for Arabic content analysis |
| **Fraud Detection** | ML systems that identify and block invalid traffic (bots, click farms, VPN masking) before brands pay for fake conversions |
| **Predictive Scoring** | Using historical data to predict future campaign performance before launch |
| **Multi-Armed Bandit** | An optimization algorithm that balances exploring new options with exploiting known best options — used for budget allocation |
| **API (Application Programming Interface)** | A set of protocols that allows different software systems to communicate — enables brands to integrate with Perff AI programmatically |
| **SaaS (Software as a Service)** | Cloud-based software delivered via subscription pricing |
| **Microservices** | An architecture where an application is built as a collection of small, independent services |
| **CDN (Content Delivery Network)** | A network of servers that delivers content quickly by serving from locations close to the user |
| **Deep Linking** | Directing a user to a specific page or product within an app or website (not just the homepage) |

## MENA Market Terms

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| **MENA** | Middle East and North Africa — a region typically comprising 20+ countries from Morocco to Iran |

| **COD (Cash on Delivery)** | A payment method where customers pay when goods are delivered — common in MENA e-commerce |

## Fundraising Terms

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| **Pre-Seed** | The first institutional funding round; typically $500K–$5M. Perff AI's $8M reflects advanced traction |
| **Series Seed / Series A** | Subsequent funding rounds as the company scales |
| **Pre-Money Valuation** | Company's value before the investment |
| **Post-Money Valuation** | Company's value after the investment (pre-money + investment amount) |
| **SAFE** | Simple Agreement for Future Equity — a convertible instrument that converts to shares at a future priced round |
| **Convertible Note** | Debt instrument that converts to equity at a future round, typically with interest and a discount |
| **Dilution** | Reduction in existing shareholders' ownership percentage when new shares are issued |
| **Pro-Rata Rights** | An investor's right to maintain their ownership percentage in future funding rounds |
| **Liquidation Preference** | The order in which investors and shareholders get paid in an exit event |
| **Cap Table** | A table showing who owns what percentage of the company |
| **ESOP** | Employee Stock Option Pool — shares reserved for employee equity compensation |
| **Anti-Dilution** | Protection for investors if the company raises future rounds at a lower valuation |
| **Drag-Along** | A right that enables a majority of shareholders to force a sale of the company |
| **ROFR (Right of First Refusal)** | A right to match any third-party offer to purchase shares from existing shareholders |
| **Board Observer** | A non-voting attendee at board meetings — typically for smaller investors |
