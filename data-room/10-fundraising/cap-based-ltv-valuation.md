## What is Cap-Based LTV?

Cap-Based LTV is a valuation methodology where the **valuation cap** (for a SAFE) or **pre-money valuation** (for a priced round) is justified by the **total future economic value** the company can extract from its customer base and pipeline.

Unlike revenue multiples alone, Cap-Based LTV accounts for:
- **Customer quality** (retention, expansion, gross margin)
- **Acquisition efficiency** (CAC, payback period)
- **Compounding value** (NRR, network effects)
- **Proven economics** vs. projected economics

This method is especially powerful for companies like Perff AI that have **strong unit economics but are still early in revenue scale**.

## Valuation Methods — Three Approaches

### Method A: Customer LTV Multiple

Investors value companies at a **multiple of total customer base LTV**, discounted for execution risk and time.

| Scenario | Customer Base | Total LTV | Multiple | Implied Valuation |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Conservative** (existing brands only) | 50 brands | $3,350,000 | 10× | **$33,500,000** |
| **Base** (existing + 24-month pipeline) | 150 brands | $10,050,000 | 4× | **$40,200,000** |
| **Aggressive** (brands + publishers + IP) | 150 brands + 2,000 publishers | $12,120,000 | 4× | **$48,480,000** |

**Why these multiples are reasonable:**
- Conservative (10× on existing): High because these are proven, retained customers
- Base (4× on total): Standard for companies with strong retention and growth
- Aggressive (4× on total + network): Includes publisher and platform value

---

## Investor FAQ — Cap-Based LTV

**Q: Why use LTV to justify valuation instead of revenue multiples?**
**A:** Because revenue multiples alone undervalue companies with exceptional unit economics. Our 8× LTV/CAC and 5.3-month payback mean every dollar invested in growth returns $8 — that efficiency should be priced in.

**Q: How do you justify a $40M cap with only $2.49M revenue?**
**A:** The $2.49M is current trailing flow (accountant baseline). The LTV of our existing 50 brands alone is $3.35M in gross profit. Add the proven ability to acquire 100+ more brands at 8× LTV/CAC, and the total economic value exceeds $10M — supporting a $35M–$40M cap at standard multiples.

**Q: What if your LTV assumptions are wrong?**
**A:** Even at a 50% haircut on LTV ($33.5K instead of $67K), the LTV/CAC ratio is still 4× (above the 3× healthy threshold), payback is still under 12 months, and the implied valuation is still above $20M.

**Q: How reliable is the LTV data?**
**A:** Very reliable. Based on 3+ years of cohort data. The 2022 cohort is still active and expanding spend. This is observed behavior, not projection.

**Q: How does NRR factor in?**
**A:** 120% NRR means existing clients grow 20% annually without new sales effort. This compounds the LTV calculation — clients become more valuable each year, not less.

**Q: Why should I trust the 87% retention rate?**
**A:** It's measured across 3 full annual cohorts (2022, 2023, 2024). Industry average is 60–70%. Our retention is driven by performance results (8.2% CVR, 7× ROAS) and deep platform integration.

**Q: What happens to LTV if you add SaaS revenue?**
**A:** LTV increases. SaaS features ($500–$2,000/month per brand) add $6K–$24K per year on top of performance revenue, with 80%+ margins. SaaS-adjusted LTV could reach $90K+ per brand.

**Q: How does the publisher network factor into valuation?**
**A:** 870 publishers at $1,035 lifetime GP each = $900K in direct value. But the real value is the network effect: more publishers attract more brands, which attract more publishers. This flywheel is not fully captured in LTV but represents significant enterprise value.

**Q: What's the downside protection for investors at this cap?**
**A:** (1) The business is near-breakeven on revenue — not burning cash to survive. (2) LTV/CAC of 8× means growth capital is deployed efficiently. (3) Even in a bear case, the company has a path to profitability without additional capital.

**Q: How does this compare to how other startups justify valuation?**
**A:** Most pre-seed companies justify valuation on TAM and team. We justify it on **proven unit economics with 3 years of cohort data**. That's a fundamentally stronger basis.
