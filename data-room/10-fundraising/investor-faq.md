## Company & Business Model

### Q: Why is this called a "Pre-Seed" if you have $2.49M in revenue?

**A:** Perff AI has never raised outside cash. The only prior arrangement is Valu.vc (5% equity), a venture studio that contributed an estimated ~$50K in services (mentorship, fractional CXO access, legal support, investor introductions, operational tools) — no cash was invested. The founders built the company from $0 to $2.49M revenue over 3+ years entirely on founder capital and reinvested revenue. While the traction is at Series Seed or even Series A level for MENA, the round is structured as a pre-seed because it is the company's **first external cash financing**. Investors get pre-seed pricing on what is effectively Seed/Series A-level traction.

---

### Q: Why raise $8M? Why not less?

**A:** $8M is the minimum to execute the three strategic imperatives simultaneously:
1. **Build the AI moat** ($3.2M) — hiring 8+ engineers to build fraud detection v2, self-serve platform, and API
2. **Enter KSA and Egypt** ($2.8M) — two of MENA's largest markets require local teams, offices, and regulatory setup
3. **Scale commercially** ($2.0M) — BD team, marketing, and customer success to grow from 50 → 120+ brands

Raising less would force the company to sequence these efforts instead of pursuing them in parallel, extending the timeline and risking the 18–24 month first-mover window.

---

### Q: How does Perff AI make money?

**A:** Primarily through a **performance margin model**:
- A brand agrees to pay $100 per conversion (sale, lead, or action)
- Perff AI retains $25 (25% take rate) and pays the publisher $75
- This happens for every conversion — creating naturally recurring revenue

Additionally:
- **SaaS features** (launching 2026): Premium AI tools at $500–$2,000/month per brand
- **Managed services**: Full-service affiliate program management for enterprise brands ($3K–$8K/month)

---

### Q: What prevents a brand from going directly to publishers?

**A:** Several structural barriers:
1. **Discovery** — Brands don't know which of 870+ publishers are right for their audience
2. **Technology** — Tracking, attribution, and fraud detection require sophisticated infrastructure
3. **Publisher payments** — Managing multi-currency payouts across 10+ MENA countries is complex
4. **Optimization** — AI-driven budget allocation and performance optimization require data at scale
5. **Trust** — Publishers need reliable, on-time payments; brands need verified traffic quality
6. **Scale** — Managing 20+ publisher relationships simultaneously is a full-time job

This is exactly why affiliate networks exist globally (CJ, Rakuten, Impact.com) — Perff AI is the MENA-native version.

## Market & Competition

### Q: Why hasn't a global player like CJ Affiliate or Impact.com already entered MENA?

**A:** Several reasons:
1. **Market size** — At ~$1.5B, MENA is still "too small" for global platforms focused on the $10B+ US market
2. **Complexity** — Arabic content, local payment rails (cash on delivery, regional wallets), regulatory diversity across 20+ countries
3. **Relationships** — MENA business is relationship-driven; cold outreach doesn't work
4. **Fraud patterns** — MENA has unique fraud vectors that global models miss
5. **Prioritization** — Global players are focused on US, Europe, and APAC

This creates an **18–24 month window** for Perff AI to establish category leadership before international platforms prioritize the region.

### Q: How defensible is the AI moat?

**A:** Very defensible, for three reasons:
1. **Proprietary data** — 50M+ MENA click events, 2M+ labeled fraud events, 500K+ conversions. This dataset cannot be bought or replicated without running a MENA affiliate network at scale for 3+ years.
2. **Compounding advantage** — Every campaign makes the AI smarter. Models improve daily.
3. **Regional specificity** — MENA fraud patterns, Arabic content, regional consumer behavior — global models perform 3× worse on MENA traffic.

### Q: What happens if cookie deprecation / privacy changes affect tracking?

**A:** Perff AI is well-positioned:
- Our primary tracking uses **server-side postbacks** (not cookies)
- We support **probabilistic matching** as a fallback for cross-device attribution
- Performance marketing is actually a **beneficiary** of privacy changes — as brands move away from impression-based advertising toward trackable, conversion-based channels
- We're investing in **first-party data partnerships** with brands
