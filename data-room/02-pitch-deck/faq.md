# Perff AI — Investor FAQ

*Concise answers for investor pitch interviews. Data-room-sourced (Feb 2026).*

## 🔥 Top Investor Questions

- **Q: What problem are you solving?**
  **A:** MENA e-commerce is $45B but captures <2% of the $18.7B global affiliate market. Brands waste money on low-converting paid ads while lacking a trusted, fraud-free performance channel. We fix that.
  *Sources: [Research and Markets - Affiliate Marketing Platform Market $18.72B (2026), $48.92B (2032), 17.1% CAGR](https://www.researchandmarkets.com/reports/6014901/affiliate-marketing-platform-market-global), Bain & Company/Google (MENA E-Commerce Report, 2024)*

- **Q: Can this be a billion-dollar company?**
  **A:** Yes. MENA affiliate TAM is $30B and growing at 17%+ annually. Impact.com ($1.5B), CJ Affiliate, and Awin all reached $1B+ serving mature markets. MENA is earlier-stage with higher growth — a category-defining player here has a clear path to $1B+.
  *Sources: [Research and Markets - Global affiliate market growing 17.1% CAGR to $48.92B by 2032](https://www.researchandmarkets.com/reports/6014901/affiliate-marketing-platform-market-global), Crunchbase (Impact.com valuation data)*

- **Q: Why you? Why this team?**
  **A:** Rima Hani built MENA affiliate from scratch (10+ yrs, built this to $1.8M with zero funding). Fahad Alsaedi is a technical co-founder with a prior exit (ex-Nana). Sadah Chanti has 17+ years in digital marketing. The three cover product, tech, and commercial — and have worked together 3+ years.

- **Q: Why should I invest now, not later?**
  **A:** We've self-funded to $1.8M revenue, 50+ brands, 870+ publishers — all with zero external capital. This round fuels the platform shift (managed → self-serve SaaS). Early investors get in at pre-seed pricing on what's effectively seed/Series A traction.

- **Q: What keeps you up at night?**
  **A:** (1) A global player like Impact or CJ making a serious MENA push. (2) Talent scarcity for senior AI/ML engineers in the region. (3) Regulatory changes in KSA or UAE.

- **Q: What's your burn rate?**
  **A:** ~$120K/month ($1.44M/year). Team is 9 people across Abu Dhabi, Amman, Riyadh, Gaza — very capital-efficient.

- **Q: What if you run out of money?**
  **A:** This round gives 28–36 months runway. Even without it, the business is near-breakeven ($1.8M revenue vs. $1.44M costs). We can throttle growth and survive on revenue alone.

- **Q: What's the path to profitability?**
  **A:** Near-breakeven today. Post-raise, we invest heavily to scale (burn increases to ~$395K/mo by Q4 2026). Target near-breakeven again at $8–9M ARR by end of 2027.

- **Q: Why haven't you raised money before?**
  **A:** Deliberate choice. We bootstrapped to prove the model works, built real revenue, and validated demand. Only Valu.vc holds 5% for studio services — no cash was taken. We raise now to accelerate, not to survive.

- **Q: What milestones does this $8M unlock?**
  **A:** 6 months: team 9→20, self-serve dashboard beta, KSA office, $250K MRR. 12 months: fraud v2 (98% detection), 80+ brands, API launch, $4M+ ARR. 24 months: Egypt entry, 2,000+ publishers, SaaS tier, $8–9M ARR, Series A–ready.

- **Q: What if a big player (Impact, CJ) enters MENA aggressively?**
  **A:** They've had years and haven't. MENA needs Arabic-first UX, local payments (AED, SAR, EGP), regional fraud models, and Net-15 publisher payouts. Rebuilding a global platform for MENA is slow; our network effects and local data deepen every month.

- **Q: How did you get your first customers?**
  **A:** Founder-led sales. Rima's 10+ year network in MENA affiliate/performance marketing opened doors to noon, Talabat, DAMAC, and others. Now 45% direct outreach, 35% referrals, 20% inbound.

- **Q: Who else is investing? Do you have a lead?**
  **A:** Actively in conversations; seeking a lead investor at $2–4M. Minimum ticket $250K. Details shared under NDA.

- **Q: What are the deal terms?**
  **A:** $8M raise, $32–40M pre-money, priced equity (preferred) or SAFE (negotiable). 1× non-participating liquidation preference. 5% ESOP pool. Board: 2 founders + 1–2 investor reps + 1 independent. Standard pro-rata, ROFR, and information rights.

## Company & Vision

- **Q: What does Perff AI do in one sentence?**
  **A:** AI-powered affiliate marketing platform for MENA — matches brands with publishers, tracks conversions, blocks fraud, and optimizes performance.

- **Q: Where are you based?**
  **A:** Delaware C-Corp (Perff Inc.) with UAE Free Zone entity; offices in Abu Dhabi, Amman, Riyadh, Gaza. KSA and Egypt entities planned.

- **Q: What's the long-term vision?**
  **A:** The category-defining self-serve affiliate platform for MENA, then expand to adjacent regions (Turkey, SEA, Africa).

- **Q: What's your moat?**
  **A:** MENA-specific data from 250+ campaigns, 870+ verified publishers with 88% retention, and AI models trained on regional fraud patterns and consumer behavior. Network effects compound with every campaign.

## Product & Technology

- **Q: What's live today vs. planned?**
  **A:** Live: fraud detection v1, predictive scoring, budget optimization, brand & publisher dashboards. Planned: fraud v2 (98%+ detection), self-serve dashboard, public API, SaaS tier.

- **Q: What's the AI actually doing?**
  **A:** Four things: (1) fraud detection — blocks fake conversions in real time, (2) predictive scoring — forecasts campaign ROI, (3) publisher matching — recommends best publishers per brand, (4) budget optimization — dynamically allocates spend across campaigns.

- **Q: How reliable is the platform?**
  **A:** 99.7% uptime, enterprise-grade security (AES-256, TLS 1.3, RBAC, 2FA).

## Traction

- **Q: What traction do you have?**
  **A:** 50+ enterprise brands (noon, Talabat, DAMAC, Temu, eyewa), 870+ publishers, 250+ campaigns, $50M+ attributed sales.

- **Q: Core metrics?**
  **A:** CVR 8.2%, ROAS ~7×, brand retention 87%, publisher retention 88%, NRR ~120%.
  *Sources: Internal analytics (see [key-metrics.md](../06-traction/key-metrics.md)). Industry benchmarks: CJ Affiliate (1-3% CVR avg), OpenView Partners (NRR 110-130% best-in-class SaaS)*

- **Q: Customer concentration risk?**
  **A:** No. No single client exceeds 15% of revenue. Spread across 9 verticals.

- **Q: Can you share a case study?**
  **A:** noon: 4× revenue growth. Talabat: 2.8× leads via coupon publishers. DAMAC: $180 CPL (vs. $300+ paid). eyewa: zero to $16K/mo in 5 months.

- **Q: How do you retain publishers?**
  **A:** Net-15 payments (industry is Net-60–90), transparent dashboards, AI-matched campaigns, and dedicated support. Result: 88% retention, 64% organic referral growth.

## Business Model

- **Q: How do you make money?**
  **A:** Performance margin on affiliate commissions (90%+ of revenue), managed services (~7%), setup fees (~2%). SaaS tier launching 2026.

- **Q: Take rate?**
  **A:** 20–30%, average 25%.

- **Q: Average contract value?**
  **A:** ~$24K per brand per year, growing 40% YoY as clients expand spend.

- **Q: Can you scale without losing margins?**
  **A:** Yes. Publisher payouts are variable and scale linearly. Team and tech costs are semi-fixed. Gross margin holds at 66–70% as we grow. SaaS tier (launching 2026) will push blended margin higher.

## Competition

- **Q: Who are the competitors?**
  **A:** Global: CJ, Rakuten, Impact ($1.5B), Awin. Regional: ArabyAds, Admitad. None are MENA-native AI-first platforms.

- **Q: Why do you win?**
  **A:** 8.2% CVR (vs. 1–3% industry), MENA-specific fraud models (95%+ detection), Arabic-first UX, local currency payouts, and Net-15 publisher payments.
  *Sources: Internal analytics vs. Influencer Marketing Hub (Affiliate Marketing Benchmark Report, 1-3% industry CVR), CHEQ (State of Fake Traffic Report, MENA fraud 3-5× higher). See [competitive-analysis.md](../04-market-research/competitive-analysis.md)*

- **Q: What if ArabyAds or Admitad copies you?**
  **A:** ArabyAds is ad-network focused, not pure affiliate. Admitad's MENA presence is thin and declining. Our 870+ publisher network with 88% retention creates switching costs that are hard to replicate.
